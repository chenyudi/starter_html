Guacamole

Origin: Mexican Source: Family Recipe Category: Appetizer

My professors asked us to make the Guacamole in the first class to taste it because it's easy to prepare and can be customized to suit different tastes.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 Roma (plum) tomatoes, diced
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 jalapeño peppers, minced (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness)
  • Juice of 1-2 limes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Recipe Steps

  1. Prepare the Avocados: Cut the avocados in half, remove the seeds, and scoop the flesh into a mixing bowl.
  2. Mash the Avocados: Use a fork or potato masher to mash the avocado to your desired level of chunkiness.
  3. Add the Aromatics: Add the finely diced onion, minced garlic, and minced jalapeño peppers to the mashed avocado.
  4. Add the Tomatoes: Gently fold in the diced tomatoes. If you prefer a less watery guacamole, you can remove the seeds and pulp from the tomatoes before dicing.
  5. Season: Squeeze the lime juice over the mixture to add flavor and help prevent the avocados from browning. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  6. Add Cilantro: Fold in the chopped fresh cilantro for a burst of freshness and flavor.
  7. Optional Seasonings: If desired, you can add ground cumin, chili powder, or paprika for additional flavor.
  8. Chill and Serve: Cover the guacamole with plastic wrap, ensuring the wrap is in direct contact with the guacamole to minimize browning. Refrigerate for about 30 minutes to let the flavors meld.

BLAT Sandwich

Origin: American Source: Family Recipe Category: Main

My host family always preferred to make BLAT sandwiches for me during high school. It is a delicious variation of the classic BLT sandwich, with the addition of avocado.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 4 slices of bread (common choices include white, wheat, or sourdough)
  • 8 slices of bacon, cooked until crispy
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 large tomato, thinly sliced
  • Lettuce leaves (such as iceberg or romaine)
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe Steps

  1. Cook the Bacon: In a skillet or on a griddle over medium heat, cook the bacon slices until they are crispy and browned. Place them on paper towels to drain excess grease.
  2. Prepare the Avocado: Cut the ripe avocado in half, remove the pit, and slice the avocado flesh into thin slices.
  3. Slice the Tomato: Slice the large tomato into thin rounds. Sprinkle the tomato slices with a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance their flavor.
  4. Assemble the Sandwich.

Stewed eggs with Minced Meat

Origin: Hong Kong Source: Family Recipe Category: Main

My family cooked stewed eggs with minced meat during dinner to taste it together because it is a tasty and comforting dish that is popular in many cuisines.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 4-6 large eggs
  • 200 grams (about 7 ounces) of ground meat
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions or cilantro for garnish (optional)

Recipe Steps

  1. Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover them with cold water. Bring the water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the eggs simmer for about 9-12 minutes for hard-boiled eggs or less time for softer yolks.
  3. After cooking, transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water to cool quickly.
  4. Once cooled, peel the eggs and set them aside.
  5. In a separate skillet or pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  6. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become fragrant and translucent about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add the ground meat and cook until it's browned and cooked through, breaking it into small pieces as it cooks.
  8. Stir in the soy sauce, sugar, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
  9. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  10. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  11. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the broth to reduce slightly and thicken.
  12. Gently place the peeled eggs into the simmering meat and broth mixture. Spoon some of the liquid over the eggs to coat them.
  13. Continue to simmer for an additional 5-7 minutes, allowing the eggs to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  14. Spoon the meat and sauce over the eggs.

Chicken Masala

Origin: Indian Source: Family Recipe Category: Main

My sister learned Chicken Masala in her school which is a flavorful and aromatic Indian dish that features tender chicken cooked in a rich and spiced tomato-based sauce.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste (a mixture of minced ginger and garlic)
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (adjust for spiciness)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (adjust for spiciness)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes or 3-4 fresh tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or coconut milk (for a creamy version)

Recipe Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, ground turmeric, ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika or chili powder, and salt.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or if time permits, marinate for a few hours or overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or pan over medium heat.
  4. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it turns soft and translucent, about 4-5 minutes.
  5. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw aroma disappears.
  6. Stir in the ground cumin, ground coriander, ground turmeric, paprika or chili powder, and garam masala. Cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing the spices to bloom.
  7. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned.
  8. Pour in the crushed tomatoes or tomato puree and mix well. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  9. Garnish the Chicken Masala with fresh cilantro leaves.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan bread, or roti (Indian flatbread).

Coca-Cola Chicken Wings

Origin: Jinan, Shandong Source: My Recipe Category: Main

I learned how to cook Coca-Cola chicken wings during the summer because it is a sweet and savory dish with a unique flavor.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 pounds (about 900 grams) chicken wings, separated into drumettes and flats
  • 1 can (12 ounces) Coca-Cola (or any cola soda)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated or minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Recipe Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the Coca-Cola, soy sauce, ketchup, brown sugar, minced garlic, grated ginger, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Mix until the sugar is dissolved and the ingredients are well combined.
  2. Add the chicken wings to the marinade, making sure they are fully coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to marinate.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Remove the chicken wings from the marinade and shake off any excess liquid. Reserve the marinade for later use.
  5. Place the chicken wings in the hot skillet and cook them until they are browned on all sides and cooked through, about 10-15 minutes.
  6. While the chicken is cooking, pour the reserved marinade into a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat and simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes, or until it thickens and reduces by about half. Stir occasionally.
  8. Once the chicken wings are cooked and crispy, remove them from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  9. Pour the thickened Coca-Cola sauce over the cooked chicken wings.
  10. Garnish the Coca-Cola chicken wings with sesame seeds and chopped green onions (if desired).
